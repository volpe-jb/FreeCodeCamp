Evelyn Boyd Granville

Life Events

  • Evelyn Boyd was born in Washington, D.C on May 1, 1924
  • Raised by her mother and aunt
  • Graduated as valedictorian at Dunbar High School, in Washington D.C.
  • She majored in mathematics and physics
  • Attended and graduated summa cum laude from Smith College in 1945
  • Evelyn attended Yale College for her graduate work
  • She received a Smith Student Aid Society scholarship for continuing her studies
  • Studied functional analysis under the supervision of Einar Hille
  • Completed her masters in 1946
  • Completed her doctorate (Ph.D) in 1949
  • Her dissertation was "On Laguerre Series in the Complex Domain"
  • In 1950, she began teaching at Fisk University, a historically black college in Nashville, Tennessee
  • Worked at Diamond Ordnance Fuze Laboratories from 1952-56
  • She began work at IBM as a computer programmer in 1957
  • Moved to Los Angeles in 1960 and worked as an IBM employee for the U.S. Space Technology Laboratories
  • Moved to the North American Aviation Space and Information Systems Division in 1962, also as an IBM employee
  • While at NAASISD, Evelyn worked on the Apollo program
  • She became a math professor at California State University, Los Angeles in 1967
  • Evelyn retired from CSULA in 1984 and moved to Texas
  • In her retirement, she taught at Texas College in Tyler, Texas for four years
  • In 1990 joined the University of Texas at Tyler as the Sam A. Lindsey Professor of mathematics
  • In 1999, the United States National Academy of Sciences inducted her into its Portrait Collection of African-Americans in Science
